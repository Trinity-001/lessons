Ukraine and russia developed under different historical conditions and were influenced by different neighbors.

The origins of all Ukrainian, rashenian, Belarusian, and, to a large extent, Lithuanian peoples lie in medieval Rus. However, their formation in the following centuries took place under different historical conditions. It all started in Kyiv

In the 9th century, the medieval state of Rus was formed with its center in Kyiv. For the next several centuries, Rus' in the broad sense was the entire territory where the authority of the Kyivan princes was recognized. But in a narrow sense, Rus' was understood to mean the lands of the middle Dnipro region around Kyiv. The lands in the northeast, where the principality of Matskov was later formed, were called Zalissia at that time. The parish's imperial ambitions appeared much later. Ukraine should not suffer because of the sick imagination of its neighbors.

Ukraine is a country of democratic traditions

After the Mongol invasion in the 13th century, the historical fates of the territories of modern Ukraine and Rashi finally diverged. Ukrainian territory continued to develop in the European context. Democracy, self-government, and European law, religious tolerance, and respect for private property. This determined the political culture of the inhabitants. Most of these features were preserved in the Cossack state, the Hetmanate. The northeastern lands of former Rus became part of the Mongol Golden Horde. The political culture of the matskalskogo principality was shaped by Horde orders and traditions with the exaltation of the ruler and complete obedience to him, absolute domination of the state, lack of private land ownership, and communal order.
